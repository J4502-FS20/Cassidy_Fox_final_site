COVID-19 Education

Since COVID-19 hit Columbia, Missouri in March of 2020 everything has changed, including education. Now, our K-12 students are forced to learn at home on thier own technical devices. For a semester, my team and I followed Columbia Public Schools, it's teachers and students to learn how they adapting. In this series we dive into the hardships of first-year teachers, parents searching for other options and the mental health of students.

Caption: Rock Bridge High School building in Columbia, MO.

Installment One

Teachers finally get to meet their students in-person

In August of 2020 Columbia Public Schools decided to start K-12 students remotely. This meant teachers were preparing lessons and teaching their students behind a screen. Maggie Robbins, a kindergarten teacher, tells us her experiences being virtual and her excitement to go back in person.

Installment Two

Parents search for in-person learning instruction

CPS made the decision to keep students online Nov. 9 and many parents aren't happy with the desicion. Instead of waiting for CPS to move in-person, parents are seeking transfers to private schools for their children in hopes of better education by in-person learning.

Installment Three

COMING SOON: Students face changes in nutrition, socialization and mental health since learning from home

Many students never expected to stay at home and learn for 6 months. But not only are lunch times and recess taken away,summers were spent friend-less too. Minimal socialization and changing routines can affect students mental health and behavior.
